Parthian Chicken from Ancient Rome

The History Behind the Dish

The recipe is dated to have come from the fourth century AD in a book called De Re Conquinaria. It was popular during its time and before.

Ingredients

  • 1 chicken (3 - 4 lbs/ 1.5 kgs)
  • 1.5 teaspoons of black pepper for cooking and garnish
  • 1 tablespoons of chopped lovage or celery leaf
  • 1.5 teaspoons of caraway seeds
  • 3 tablespoons of garum/liquamen (fish sauce)
  • 1 cups of wine, red or white
  • .75 teaspoons of asafoetida powder
  • .25 cups of water

The Steps to Take

  1. Preheat oven to 450F or 230C.
  2. Break the chicken in half at the backbone (spatchcock) and place into a cooking container.
  3. Mix the lovage, pepper, caraway, garum, and wine into a mixing bowl and pour it over the chicken.
  4. Dissolve the asafoetida in water in a separate bowl and also pour it over the chicken.
  5. Bake the chicken for 40-45 minutes or until a food thermonitor reads 160-165F or 72-74C.
  6. When the chicken is done, remove it from the oven and let it rest for 10 minutes.
  7. Serve with the cooking liquids and pepper garnish.
